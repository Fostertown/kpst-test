  • Q1: Galactic vs Nuanced Themes

    Does most of your content address really big themes or do you tackle complex topics?

    Answer Key:

    • Star Wars! The dark side versus the light side. Like Star Wars, your content tends to address broad, overarching topics.
    • Star Trek! Star Trek explores a lot of specific themes (intergalactic race relations, wormholes, cultural assimilation). Just like the series, you embrace rather than shun complex concepts.

  • Q2: Brave New World vs Forward-Thinking

    Does your content shift the audience's perspective about their current market or does it focus on where that market is headed?

    Answer Key:

    • Star Wars! “In a galaxy far, far away…” Your content transports the audience to a different, better world - for their business that is.
    • Star Trek! It’s warp drive to the future. Your content explores futuristic topics and how to prepare for the vast unknown.

  • Q3: Competitive Content vs Investigative Content

    Do you focus your content efforts on equipping customers to best competitors or do you spend more time exploring emerging trends and themes?

    Answer Key:

    • Star Wars! It’s the Empire against the Rebel Alliance. Like Star Wars, your content is about beating the competition.
    • Star Trek! Star Trek sought out “new life and new civilizations.” Similarly, your content likes to explore new trends and themes.

  • Q4: Sleek vs Gritty

    When it comes to visuals, do you prefer to use super modern design or visceral true-to-life imagery?

    Answer Key:

    • Star Trek! Like the bridge of the Enterprise, your visual strategy follows a smooth style.
    • Star Wars! Like the Millennium Falcon, your visual strategy is as real as it gets.

  • Q5: Flat Decision-Makers vs Clear Hierarchies

    When crafting your content, do you largely target companies with a flat approach to decision-making or those with very clear organizational charts?

    Answer Key:

    • Star Wars! We wouldn’t call Yoda the boss (more like a mentor). Your content is meant to reach organizations where titles mean very little.
    • Star Trek! In Star Trek, you need the ear of the Captain. Your content is designed to influence the Captain - or even the Admiral - at organizations.

  • Q6: Market Forces vs Internal Challenges

    Are you more concerned with creating content that helps the audience understand and adapt to outside market forces or preparing them to deal with conflicts and challenges inside their organizations?

    Answer Key:

    • Star Trek! Be it “Khan!” or the Borg, Star Trek characters battle a lot of outside forces. Your content is meant to help prospects and customers overcome external, industry challenges.
    • Star Wars! Anakin and Luke Skywalker both waged internal battles. Your content is really meant to help professionals overcome internal obstacles and conflicts.
